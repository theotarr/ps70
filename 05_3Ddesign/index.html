Week 5 — 3D Design, Printing, and Scanning

Tesseract Design in Fusion

This week I made a tesseract (a 3D shadow of a 4-dimensional cube), ie. a cube inside of a cube.

Design Screenshots from Fusion

Printing and Post-Processing

  • Print run — About 45 minutes. Layers behaved, corners stayed down. I’ll take the win.
  • Support cleanup — Minimal tree supports popped off with pliers. I love when that happens.
  • Finishing pass — Light sanding on the touch points and a matte finish to make the edges read cleanly.

Final Result

The print nails the nested-cube illusion—your eyes bounce between the frame and the inner cube while the connectors quietly do the storytelling. It’s a small desk-sized reminder that higher-dimensional stuff can look great on a humble FDM printer.

3D Scanning: Flipper Zero

I scanned my Flipper Zero using the lab's 3D scanner. It took a few tries to get usable results, since the device's reflective screen and infrared emitter made it tricky for the scanner to pick up certain details cleanly.

To make the scanned mesh viewable in the online viewer (viewstl.com), I had to reduce the size of the STL file below 30 MiB. For that, I used the Fast Quadric Mesh Simplification tool, which lets you quickly downscale STL/OBJ models in the browser [source](https://myminifactory.github.io/Fast-Quadric-Mesh-Simplification/).
